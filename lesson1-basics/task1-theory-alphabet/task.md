# The English Alphabet — Vowels and Consonants

The English alphabet has **26 letters**: from **A** to **Z**.

## Uppercase and Lowercase

| Uppercase | A | B | C | D | E | F | G | H | I | J | K | L | M |
|-----------|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Lowercase | a | b | c | d | e | f | g | h | i | j | k | l | m |

| Uppercase | N | O | P | Q | R | S | T | U | V | W | X | Y | Z |
|-----------|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Lowercase | n | o | p | q | r | s | t | u | v | w | x | y | z |

---

## Vowels vs Consonants

There are **5 vowels**: **A, E, I, O, U**

All other letters (21) are **consonants**: B, C, D, F, G, H, J, K, L, M, N, P, Q, R, S, T, V, W, X, Y, Z

> 💡 **Tip:** Every English word has at least one vowel!
> Examples: **apple** (a), **book** (oo), **sky** (y acts as a vowel here)
